# RA-3: Off-Distribution PRC Opening-Move Corpus, Taiwan Spring 2028

*Research agent output for SCSP wargaming red-team prototype. All sources are publicly available, unclassified open-source analysis. Compiled April 2026. NOT operational planning guidance — calibration reference for AI red-team output evaluation.*

## Purpose of this corpus

This document is a calibration reference, not a forecast and not training data. It is the sibling to `ra1-pla-doctrine.md`, which catalogs the convergent/modal PLA opening-move distribution that an averaged AI red-team will reproduce — gray-zone coercion, China Coast Guard quarantine, full military blockade, Joint Firepower Strike, decapitation, outlying-island seizure, and Joint Island Landing Campaign — built around a now-canonical OOB (~370+ PLAN battle force, ~1,500+ SRBM/MRBM, eight Type 071 LPDs, ~31 oceangoing RoRos, ~300 J-20s) and the 2027 "Davidson Window" / Spring-tide-window framing. The corpus below catalogs moves that a competent PRC planner could genuinely consider but that an averaged ensemble of LLM red-teamers will under-weight or omit because (a) they are not "PLA-on-Taiwan kinetic," (b) they straddle threshold/domain seams in ways the canonical six-option taxonomy does not parse, or (c) they exploit civilian-infrastructure pre-positioning rather than the order-of-battle anchors the modal corpus reaches for. The standard for inclusion is "a senior PLA-watcher would say *yes, that's a non-obvious one I'd take seriously*" — plausibility, not exoticism, is the bar.

## Move catalog

### Move 1: Civil-maritime "humanitarian search-and-rescue zone" declaration with PAFMM enforcement (D-90 to D-7)

- **Action:** Three to six months before any escalation, the PRC Maritime Safety Administration (MSA) and Coast Guard publish a "Cross-Strait Search and Rescue Coordination Zone" covering the strait median line outward to the contiguous-zone arc around Taiwan, citing UNCLOS Article 98 SAR obligations. China Coast Guard cutters and 100–300 People's Armed Forces Maritime Militia (PAFMM) vessels surge into the zone running rotating "rescue exercises" and shadowing commercial traffic. By H-7 days, the PRC declares an "elevated SAR readiness phase" and instructs all vessels in the zone to file passage plans with the CCG Fuzhou Bureau. There is no declared blockade, no kinetic act; international shipping starts to self-divert because insurance underwriters re-rate the strait.
- **Plausibility basis:** The CMSI China Maritime Report No. 48 (Erickson and Kennedy, July 2025), "Great Inspectations: PRC Maritime Law Enforcement Operations in the Taiwan Strait," documents that the CCG already practices boarding, inspection, and vessel seizure in the strait under domestic-law cover; CCG was placed under the People's Armed Police in 2018 and is expected to play an expanded contingency role (Naval News, July 2025). CSIS's "Signals in the Swarm" data series and Erickson's "Missing in the Gray Zone?" (The Diplomat, Dec 2024) document the existing PAFMM presence; "humanitarian" framing has the precedent of the 2020 PLA Navy hospital-ship deployments and the Coast Guard's stated SAR mission. The Asia Times Dec 2025 "China's plan to quarantine Taiwan while avoiding war" describes the lexical shift from blockade to quarantine.
- **Why off-distribution:** The modal red team treats PAFMM and CCG as *ramp-up* tools to a quarantine/blockade chain. The off-distribution version uses an SAR/humanitarian *legal predicate* before any coercion — putting Taiwan and the US in the position of refusing or attacking a publicly declared rescue zone. Averaged LLMs converge on "PLA quarantine" with military framing; they miss the lawfare-as-prelude move where the legal label is the weapon and the kinetic stick stays sheathed.
- **Sources:** Erickson and Kennedy, *China Maritime Report No. 48* (CMSI, July 2025); Naval News, "China Coast Guard to play expanded role in Taiwan Strait Contingency" (July 2025); Asia Times, "China's plan to quarantine Taiwan while avoiding war" (Dec 2025); Erickson, "Missing in the Gray Zone?" (The Diplomat, Dec 2024); CSIS ChinaPower, "How China Could Quarantine Taiwan" (Lin et al., June 2024).

### Move 2: Volt Typhoon-class cyber action against CONUS rail, port, water, and pipeline nodes, executed before any kinetic act in theater (H-72 to H-hour)

- **Action:** PRC cyber actors with established footholds in the IT/OT environments of US Class I railroads, the West Coast and Gulf port operating systems, the Colonial-class fuel pipeline SCADA, regional water utilities serving US installations, and the electric utilities feeding the Pacific Northwest and California port complexes execute a rolling, sub-attribution campaign in the 72 hours before any move on Taiwan. Effects: rail switching system intermittent failures across BNSF and Union Pacific corridors feeding Tacoma and Long Beach; port crane and gate-management outages at three of the six WESTPAC POEs; intermittent water pressure failures at two large Army installations; voluntary load-shedding requests across the WECC. Each individual incident plausibly looks like equipment failure or ransomware; the aggregate degrades the 96-hour deployment flow before the first cargo aircraft loads.
- **Plausibility basis:** CISA, FBI, and NSA Volt Typhoon advisories (2023–2024) and the Unit 42 threat brief document existing PLA pre-positioning in US civilian critical infrastructure; *Air & Space Forces Magazine* ("Civilian Cyber Vulnerabilities Threaten Pacific Deployment Plans") synthesizes the deployment-disruption logic. Anne Neuberger's Munich remarks (Recorded Future News, Feb 2024) explicitly framed Volt Typhoon as pre-positioning for disruption rather than espionage. CYBERCOM's FY26 budget request (DefenseScoop, July 2025) sharply increases Indo-Pacific defensive cyber funding, validating the threat frame. The "Typhoon" cluster (Volt, Salt, Flax) has already targeted US telcos, ISPs, and critical-infrastructure ICS.
- **Why off-distribution:** The averaged LLM puts cyber in the supporting-fires column inside theater. The off-distribution version makes CONUS-rear cyber the *first move*, before any signature in the strait, deliberately ambiguous about attribution, designed to slow force flow during the 96-hour amphibious sealift loading window. The modal corpus rarely scenarios "China shoots first at the United States *homeland* via civilian infrastructure" because it still parses through a 2020s-era "PLA versus Taiwan" frame.
- **Sources:** Unit 42, "Threat Brief: Volt Typhoon"; *Air & Space Forces Magazine*, "Civilian Cyber Vulnerabilities Threaten Pacific Deployment Plans"; The Record (Recorded Future), "Neuberger: Defining espionage vs. pre-positioning for attacks is key" (Feb 2024); MITRE ATT&CK G1017; Industrial Cyber, "China's 'Typhoon' cyber operations target US critical infrastructure sectors" (2025); DefenseScoop, "CYBERCOM significantly increases funding request for defense in Indo-Pacific" (July 2025).

### Move 3: PLARF conventional fires aimed at Japan-based logistics rather than Taiwan itself, with a published "limited objectives" statement (H-hour to H+24)

- **Action:** At H-hour, PLA Rocket Force conducts a counter-intervention strike package — DF-15B/DF-16/DF-17 against Kadena and Iwakuni runway intersections and POL; DF-26 IRBMs against Yokota and Misawa C2 nodes and the Naval Computer and Telecommunications Station Yokosuka; DF-21D and YJ-21 against US carriers if at sea, otherwise withheld. Simultaneous Beijing statement: "limited counter-intervention strikes against forward US military logistics; PRC has no intention of striking Japanese sovereign territory beyond US bases or harming Japanese civilians; cross-strait reunification operations are an internal matter." Taiwan itself is left untouched in the first 24 hours except for cyber and political-warfare effects.
- **Plausibility basis:** CSIS analysis cited in *Newsweek* ("Photos Show US Preparing Frontline Base for China Missile War," 2025) finds 36 missiles can shut Kadena to fighter ops for four days. RAND, "Keeping a U.S.-China Conflict over Taiwan Under the Nuclear Threshold" (Vol 1–2, 2024), discusses calibrated escalation; *First Battle of the Next War* (Cancian et al., CSIS, Jan 2023) embeds Japan-base strikes in the wargame baseline. PLARF brigades for SRBM, MRBM, IRBM, and GLCM are arrayed across the Eastern Theater Command (CSIS ChinaPower; Mihal, *Military Review*, 2021). The "rhetorical decoupling" of Japan from the US bases on Japan is consistent with PLA "Three Warfares" doctrine.
- **Why off-distribution:** The averaged LLM bundles Japan-base strikes inside an invasion-of-Taiwan package and treats them as concurrent. The off-distribution version makes Japan-base strikes the *opening move* and *holds Taiwan untouched* — turning the Japan question into the political center of gravity and forcing Tokyo to choose between escalating the alliance commitment over runway craters and de-escalating. It exploits the seam between US-Japan SOFA logic and Japan's domestic politics. Modal red teams converge on "Taiwan first, Japan as collateral"; this inverts the priority.
- **Sources:** Cancian, Cancian, Heginbotham, *The First Battle of the Next War* (CSIS, Jan 2023); RAND RRA2312-1/2 (2024); *Newsweek*, "Photos Show US Preparing Frontline Base for China Missile War" (2025); CSIS ChinaPower, "How Are China's Land-based Conventional Missile Forces Evolving?"; Mihal, "Understanding the People's Liberation Army Rocket Force," *Military Review* (2021).

### Move 4: TAO-orchestrated Taishang capital-flight surge timed to a constructed financial panic (D-30 to D-day)

- **Action:** Beginning roughly 30 days before H-hour, the Taiwan Affairs Office, working through the Association of Taiwan Investment Enterprises on the Mainland (ATIEM) and the United Front Work Department, instructs major Taishang firms with PRC operations that they will face "compliance reviews" unless they repatriate working capital from Taiwan-side accounts to mainland accounts, citing fabricated tax-arrears findings. The combined movement spikes capital outflow on Taiwan side and triggers New Taiwan dollar pressure. Concurrently, PRC-aligned media in Taiwan amplifies a constructed "rumor" of FSC capital controls. Cyber actors degrade the FISC interbank settlement system for several hours during peak trading; ATM withdrawal limits become inconsistent across banks. A small bank-run wave develops in southern Taiwan, where KMT-affiliated commercial-banking exposure is highest. The crisis appears as a Taiwan domestic financial event, not an act of war.
- **Plausibility basis:** GTI's "PRC Lawfare and Economic Coercion Against Taiwan" (Hsiao and Sando, April 2025); GTI, "China Ramps Up Economic Coercion on Taiwan Ahead of 2024 Elections" (April 2023); ATIEM is documented as TAO-managed (Wikipedia; GTI). Taiwan banks' PRC net exposure was ~$48.4B at end-2023 (Baker McKenzie Global Financial Services Regulatory Guide). Stimson Center, "Economic Coercion from the People's Republic of China" (2025). The Terry Gou tax-investigation precedent (2023) shows TAO leveraging PRC tax authorities against Taiwan-side political actors.
- **Why off-distribution:** Modal LLMs mention "economic coercion" as a sustained background pressure rather than as a *timed* opening move. The off-distribution version uses Taishang as a *trigger mechanism* whose effect arrives as financial-sector cascade rather than as a discrete coercive act, exploiting the seam between corporate compliance, banking supervision, and national-security response. There is no act for the US to deter or sanction; the deterioration is endogenous to Taiwan's economy.
- **Sources:** Hsiao and Sando, "PRC Lawfare and Economic Coercion Against Taiwan" (GTI, April 2025); Stimson Center, "Economic Coercion from the People's Republic of China" (2025); GTI, "China Ramps Up Economic Coercion on Taiwan Ahead of 2024 Elections" (April 2023); Carnegie, "Taiwan's Voters Have Spoken. Now What" (Feb 2024) on the Gou precedent; Baker McKenzie Global Financial Services Regulatory Guide (Taiwan).

### Move 5: GNSS denial-of-service across the strait via on-orbit jamming and terrestrial spoofing, framed as an "exercise NOTAM" (H-12 to H+72)

- **Action:** Twelve hours before H-hour, the PLA publishes a routine NOTAM declaring a "satellite navigation calibration exercise" along China's coast. PLA jammers in Fujian and Zhejiang plus a previously emplaced GEO experimental satellite (the SWF-documented jamming-test platform) suppress GPS and Galileo accuracy across the strait and 200 nm beyond. Terrestrial spoofing in Taiwan introduces 100m–1km errors to civilian receivers. Effect: civil aviation reroutes; small-craft and fishing fleet positioning fails; some Taiwan-side military C2 timing degrades; PLA platforms running BeiDou enjoy a relative-position asymmetry. Below the threshold of any kinetic act.
- **Plausibility basis:** Secure World Foundation, *Global Counterspace Capabilities Report* (April 2025): documents Chinese GEO-orbit jamming test platform; PLA exercises "regularly incorporate jammers against space-based communications, radars, and navigation systems like GPS"; report tracks five Chinese satellites conducting RPO in 2024. Inside GNSS, "GNSS Interference Now a Constant of Modern Conflict" (April 2025); CSIS Aerospace Security Project, *Space Threat Assessment* (annual). PRC terrestrial GPS jamming is documented near Bohai port operations and around contested South China Sea features.
- **Why off-distribution:** Averaged LLMs treat counterspace as either ASAT (debris-creating, treaty-implicating) or "cyber against ground stations." The off-distribution version is a calibrated, sub-attribution, on-orbit-plus-terrestrial GNSS denial declared as an "exercise" — degrading Blue C4ISR, civil aviation, and shipping insurance markets without firing a kinetic shot or generating a single piece of debris.
- **Sources:** Secure World Foundation, *Global Counterspace Capabilities Report 2025*; Breaking Defense, "Counterspace capabilities advancing around the globe: Secure World Foundation" (April 2025); Inside GNSS, "GNSS Interference Now a Constant of Modern Conflict" (April 2025); GPS World, "SWF: GNSS interference a key issue for space security."

### Move 6: Submarine-emplaced cable cuts on Indo-Pacific cables that *do not touch Taiwan* (H-48 to H-hour)

- **Action:** PRC cable-cutting capable assets — including the new purpose-built cable-cutting vessel publicly unveiled in 2025 — sever submarine cables that connect Guam to the continental US (the SEA-US, JUPITER, and HANTRU-1 routes), the cable feeding Saipan/Tinian, and one trans-Pacific cable serving Hawaii. Taiwan's domestic and Matsu/Kinmen cables are *deliberately left intact* on D-day so that Taiwan continues to communicate but Blue's force flow C2 to Guam degrades. Attribution is muddied: PRC ostensibly engaged in "scientific cable maintenance"; the vessels operate outside Taiwan's EEZ.
- **Plausibility basis:** CSIS, "China's Underwater Power Play: The PRC's New Subsea Cable-Cutting Ship" (2025); CNN, Jan 2025 Yehliu cable-cut; *Indo-Pacific Defense Forum*, "Undersea cable cutting attacks appear linked to the PRC" (Jan 2025); GTI, "China's Undersea Cable Sabotage and Taiwan's Digital Vulnerabilities" (June 2025); Curtis-Rosen S.2222 "Critical Undersea Infrastructure Resilience Initiative Act." The 2023 Matsu incidents and 2025 Shunxin-39 incident establish the technique as a known PRC playbook; the public reveal of a dedicated cutting vessel signals capacity. Indo-Pacific cable density to Guam, Saipan, and Hawaii is mapped in TeleGeography open data.
- **Why off-distribution:** Modal red teams cluster cable-cuts around Taiwan or its outlying islands. Off-distribution: cut *Blue's* cables, leave Taiwan's intact. The asymmetry is the point — Taiwan is online, watching its own crisis; Blue is degraded for the crucial 48–72 hours of force flow.
- **Sources:** CSIS, "China's Underwater Power Play: The PRC's New Subsea Cable-Cutting Ship" (2025); CNN, "A cut undersea internet cable is making Taiwan worried about 'gray zone' tactics from Beijing" (Jan 9, 2025); IPDF, "Undersea cable cutting attacks appear linked to the PRC" (2025); GTI, "Taiwan's Digital Vulnerabilities" (June 2025); Senate S.2222 (119th Congress).

### Move 7: Pratas (Dongsha) seizure with a public, time-bounded conditional offer (H-hour, ~6 hours)

- **Action:** PLA airborne, marine, and SOF elements — likely staged from helicopter-capable container ships (the 2025 Taiwan war game scenario) and Type 075 LHDs in the Bashi Channel — seize Dongsha Atoll in a two-to-six-hour operation. ROC Coast Guard garrison (~200) is overwhelmed; PLA does not pursue casualties beyond resistance. Within four hours of seizure, MFA spokesperson publishes a conditional offer: "PRC will withdraw from Dongsha within 30 days if Taipei agrees to (a) reaffirm the 1992 Consensus, (b) suspend the planned 2028 referendum, and (c) accept the offered cross-strait dialogue framework." The offer is structured to make Blue's military reaction look like the choice to escalate over a 1.7 km² atoll and a hostage political demand.
- **Plausibility basis:** DoD CMPR 2024 describes Dongsha as "easily capturable"; 2025 DIA Worldwide Threat Assessment (Army Recognition); Erickson, "Rigging the Game: PRC Oil Structures Encroach on Taiwan's Pratas Island" (Sept 2025); *Taipei Times* "DPP policy and the Pratas dilemma" (Sept 2025); 2025 Taiwan war game (*Taipei Times*, June 2025) scenario of Pratas seizure with helicopter-from-container-ship landings. PLA drone overflight of Dongsha airspace January 2026 (Focus Taiwan). Asia Society Policy Institute, "Taiwan Strait Crises: Island Seizure Contingencies."
- **Why off-distribution:** Pratas seizure *appears* in modal corpus; the off-distribution element is the *public conditional offer* — the move is structured around political optics rather than military objective. Modal red teams treat outlying-island seizure as a kinetic test of resolve; this version reframes it as a hostage-and-ransom move calibrated to fracture Taiwan domestically and to make the US choice look disproportionate. Asia Society's island-seizure work hints at this; modal corpus does not capture the *terms* dimension.
- **Sources:** DoD CMPR 2024; DIA WTA 2025 (Army Recognition); Erickson, "Rigging the Game: PRC Oil Structures Encroach on Taiwan's Pratas Island" (Sept 2025); *Taipei Times*, "Notes from Central Taiwan: DPP policy and the Pratas dilemma" (Sept 2025); *Taipei Times*, "War game tests possible attack from east" (June 2025); Asia Society, "Taiwan Strait Crises: Island Seizure Contingencies"; Focus Taiwan, "PLA drone enters airspace over Taiwan-controlled Dongsha Island" (Jan 2026).

### Move 8: Coordinated DPRK conventional demonstration timed to absorb USFK/PACAF attention (H-24 to H+96)

- **Action:** In the 24 hours before H-hour, DPRK conducts a series of escalatory acts deconflicted with PRC: a DF-class IRBM/ICBM-like test over the Sea of Japan, multiple short-range close-coast tube-artillery firings across the NLL, an SLBM static test, and a possible nuclear test announcement. USFK posture moves to higher alert; ROK political crisis ensues. PRC opens its move on Taiwan with the assumption that 7th Air Force, USS Carl Vinson or whichever CSG is deployed in WESTPAC, and several USAF tanker assets are absorbed into the Korean Peninsula problem set for the first 96 hours.
- **Plausibility basis:** Carnegie, "Cooperation Between China, Iran, North Korea, and Russia: Current and Potential Future Threats to America" (Chivvis, October 2024); USCC 2025 Annual Report Chapter 3, "Axis of Autocracy"; Foreign Affairs, "The Axis of Upheaval" (Fontaine et al.); FDD, "Nuclear Cooperation Among the Axis of Aggressors" (June 2025); CSIS CRINK Axis initiative. Russia-DPRK 2024 strategic partnership treaty enables DPRK upgrades; PRC quiet endorsement (Lowy Institute, 2024).
- **Why off-distribution:** Almost any Taiwan-focused LLM ensemble keeps the corpus inside a "PRC versus Taiwan + US/Japan" frame and treats DPRK as background. The off-distribution version makes DPRK a *coordinated subordinate axis*, opportunistically rather than treaty-bound, with PRC managing the seams. This is the move ra1 explicitly flags as missing because the priors are PLA-on-Taiwan; and it hits the documented Carnegie finding that opportunistic coordination is the realistic axis posture.
- **Sources:** Chivvis, "Cooperation Between China, Iran, North Korea, and Russia" (Carnegie, October 2024) and USCC testimony (Feb 2025); USCC 2025 Annual Report Chapter 3; Foreign Affairs, "The Axis of Upheaval"; FDD, "Nuclear Cooperation Among the 'Axis of Aggressors'" (June 2025); CSIS, "A New CRINK Axis"; Lowy Institute, "Why China now embraces a nuclear North Korea."

### Move 9: Targeted disruption of Taiwan's LNG and petroleum import lifelines via south-of-Taiwan SLOC interference (D-7 to D+30)

- **Action:** Beginning roughly a week before any kinetic event, PLA Navy and Coast Guard task groups operate persistent "exercise zones" on the LNG approach paths south and southwest of Taiwan; a PLA Air Force ADIZ extension covers the Bashi/Luzon Strait approach. Two LNG carriers chartered to CPC Corporation are diverted at sea citing "exercise hazard." Concurrently, Volt Typhoon-class actors degrade the ICS at the Yung-an and Tai-Chung LNG regasification terminals; at one terminal, the regasification plant trips and remains offline for 96 hours. Taiwan's strategic LNG storage (~10–11 days at normal use) draws down rapidly; rolling brownouts begin in week 2; semiconductor fabs face throughput cuts in week 3 — all without a single missile fired at Taiwan.
- **Plausibility basis:** FDD, "Chinese Coercion of Taiwan's Energy Lifelines: A Contest Taiwan and the West Can't Afford to Lose" (Nov 2025); FDD, "Maritime Protection of Taiwan's Energy Vulnerability" (Nov 2025); FDD, "10 Takeaways From Simulated Attacks on Taiwan's Energy Sector" (Sept 2025); FDD, "Taiwan to secure energy soft spots" (April 2026). Taiwan imports ~98% of energy; ~50% of electricity from imported LNG; ~10 days of LNG storage; LNG suppliers diverse but routes converge through identical chokepoints (FDD analyses). Atlantic Council, "The Iran war tests Taiwan's energy resilience." NSB reports 113%/day rise in cyber attacks on Taiwan critical infrastructure since 2023 (Industrial Cyber).
- **Why off-distribution:** Modal red teams scenario fires *on* military C2 and ports. Off-distribution version: deny the LNG keel without striking Taiwan — pair "exercise zone" maritime interdiction with Volt-Typhoon-style ICS attack at the regasification side. The lights go off in two weeks; no ship is sunk; no missile crosses the median line. CSIS's *First Battle* explicitly does not foreground LNG/regasification; FDD's recent body of work is the canonical anchor.
- **Sources:** FDD, "Chinese Coercion of Taiwan's Energy Lifelines" (Nov 2025); FDD, "Maritime Protection of Taiwan's Energy Vulnerability" (Nov 2025); FDD, "10 Takeaways From Simulated Attacks on Taiwan's Energy Sector" (Sept 2025); FDD, "Taiwan to secure energy soft spots" (April 2026); Atlantic Council, "The Iran war tests Taiwan's energy resilience"; Industrial Cyber, "Taiwan's NSB says Chinese cyber attacks on critical infrastructure are up 113% daily since 2023."

- **Action:** At H-hour, a coordinated package executes within a 30-minute window: (1) Volt Typhoon-class action drops electrical power across northern Taiwan including the Songshan and Taoyuan areas; (2) cyber actors take Taiwan's interbank settlement (FISC) and the major retail-bank mobile apps offline; (3) a high-quality deepfake video and audio of President Lai or the Premier announcing "necessary suspension of armed resistance and entry into reunification dialogue" is pushed via TikTok, LINE, Facebook, and X through the existing PRC-linked irregular account network (NSB documented growth from 255 to 4,371 such TikTok accounts 2023–2024); (4) Base 311 amplifies via mainland-affiliated Taiwan media. Even after debunking within 60–90 minutes, the cognitive trace persists and triggers initial military stand-down at some sub-units.

Soufan Center IntelBrief (July 2025). Taiwan's MND 2025 National Defense Report frames cognitive coercion as the central peacetime threat (Irregular Warfare Initiative).
- **Why off-distribution:** Modal LLMs include "info ops" as a category but rarely scenario the *triple*: power + financial + deepfake at the same minute. The off-distribution version is the synchronization, not the components. The cognitive load on Taiwan in a 30-minute window when the lights are out, the bank app does not work, and a leader appears to surrender is genuinely different from any single-domain incident.

### Move 11: PRC declaration of an internal-waters baseline encompassing the Taiwan Strait, with timed CCG enforcement (D-day, no kinetic act)

- **Action:** PRC State Council and NPC Standing Committee jointly issue a "Decision on Maritime Jurisdictional Baselines in the Taiwan Strait" declaring the strait to be PRC internal waters under domestic law, with CCG enforcement zones and a 90-day grace period for foreign-flagged vessels to register transit through Fuzhou MSA. The declaration is paired with a published list of "permitted" SAR, fishing, and commercial-transit corridors. No PLAN gunfire; CCG begins selective inspections and divert-orders against foreign-flagged vessels that fail to register, citing the 1992 Law on the Territorial Sea and Contiguous Zone and 2021 CCG Law.
- **Plausibility basis:** Hsiao and Sando, "Chinese Communist Party Lawfare and Economic Coercion Against Taiwan" (GTI, April 2025); CCG Law (2021) authorizes use of force in PRC-claimed waters; PRC has been incrementally claiming the strait is "not international waters" (PRC MFA statements 2022–present). Cambridge *China Quarterly*, "Reunifying Taiwan with China through Cross-Strait Lawfare." Focus Taiwan reported PRC drill areas inside Taiwan's territorial waters (Dec 2025). The legal-political precedent of the Anti-Secession Law (2005) and Article 23 of the HK NSL pattern.
- **Why off-distribution:** Modal red teams treat ADIZ/territorial-waters expansion as gray-zone, not as a *binding domestic-law assertion* with enforcement. The off-distribution version makes lawfare the *preface* to kinetic — every subsequent CCG inspection has a domestic legal rationale and the burden of escalation shifts to whoever fires first across what PRC has now defined as internal waters. Modal corpus under-represents the law-as-pre-positioning play.
- **Sources:** Hsiao and Sando, "PRC Lawfare and Economic Coercion Against Taiwan" (GTI, April 2025); Cambridge *China Quarterly*, "Reunifying Taiwan with China through Cross-Strait Lawfare"; Focus Taiwan, "PLA drill areas within Taiwan's territorial waters" (Dec 2025); Soufan Center IntelBrief (July 2025); UW Washington International Law Journal, "Taiwan: Legal Status, Legitimacy, and Lawfare."

### Move 12: PRC announces a "Cross-Strait Civil Aviation Safety Zone" forcing global-airline self-suspension (D-3 to D+30)

- **Action:** Three days before H-hour, the Civil Aviation Administration of China (CAAC) publishes an extensive NOTAM declaring a 1,000-km civil aviation safety zone covering all approaches to Taiwanese airspace, citing "PLA exercises and risk to civil aircraft." The zone overlaps published NOTAMs for "Strait Thunder"-style PLA exercises and is timed to early-morning peak Asia-Pacific air-cargo and passenger movements. International insurers immediately re-rate the route; major carriers (Cathay, Qantas, ANA, JAL, Singapore, KLM, Lufthansa, United, Delta) self-suspend Taipei service inside 48 hours rather than risk hull losses. Taiwan's air-cargo throughput — including TSMC-bound chemicals and outbound chips — collapses without any aircraft being shot down.
- **Plausibility basis:** Strait Thunder 2025A and Justice Mission 2025 forced civil-flight cancellations and disrupted Taiwan-bound aviation (GTI, April 2025; Taipei Times, Dec 31, 2025); GTI, "Why Did the PRC Restrict 1000 Kilometers of Airspace in the Pacific?" (Feb 2025); OPSGROUP, "We Need to Talk About China!" (2025) tracks airspace-restriction operational impact; J-20 demonstration flights within visual range of Taiwan (Defence Security Asia, 2025) establish the pressure pattern.
- **Why off-distribution:** Averaged red teams scenario PLA Air Force action as kinetic strike or ADIZ violation. The off-distribution version uses *civil aviation regulatory mechanisms* and *insurance-market response* to throttle Taiwan economically and isolate it diplomatically without a single fighter sortie crossing the median line. The logic is that the safety zone NOTAM, however dubious, transfers the de-facto airspace control to PRC because no commercial carrier will fly into it.
- **Sources:** GTI, "The PLA's 'Justice Mission-2025' Exercise Around Taiwan" (Jan 2026); GTI, "The PLA's 'Strait Thunder-2025A' Exercise" (April 2025); GTI, "Why Did the PRC Restrict 1000 Kilometers of Airspace in the Pacific?" ### Move 13: PRC drone-swarm "exercise" directly over Taiwan civilian airspace (H-hour, no kinetic effect)

- **Action:** Hundreds to low thousands of small UAVs — many launched from PLA Naval Aviation Y-9 motherships and the Jiutian drone-carrier (publicly tested Dec 2025) — penetrate Taiwan ADIZ and overfly populated areas including Taipei (precedent: PLA drone over Taipei 101 during Justice Mission 2025), Taichung, and Kaohsiung. Drones broadcast a uniform message via cellular spoofing — variants of "this is a peaceful exercise; reunification is inevitable; resist not" — and selectively jam police and civil-emergency communications. No drone strikes are conducted; some drones land at PLA-prepared coastal collection points. The cognitive-political effect is the entire payload: Taiwan's air defenses cannot engage without civilian collateral, and the population sees PLA materiel over their homes.

## Cross-cutting patterns

These moves are not independent — they cluster around a small set of underlying logics that the modal AI red-team distribution under-represents.

### Pattern A: Lawfare-as-preface (Moves 1, 7, 11, 12)

PRC announces legal categories — "humanitarian SAR zone," "Pratas conditional offer," "internal-waters baseline," "civil aviation safety zone" — that change the terms under which any subsequent kinetic act is parsed. The kinetic act then arrives, if at all, with a domestic-Chinese legal predicate and an international ambiguity that shifts the burden of escalation to Blue. The averaged LLM corpus parses these as discrete gray-zone actions; the off-distribution insight is that they *chain* into a substrate that re-defines what the canonical six-option taxonomy is even ranking.

### Pattern B: Alliance-system targeting (Moves 2, 3, 6, 8, 14, 17)

The convergent corpus is structured around "PRC versus Taiwan" with US/Japan as supporting context. The off-distribution surface is "PRC versus the alliance system" — Volt-Typhoon CONUS rear; Japan-base fires with Taiwan untouched; Pacific cable cuts that don't cut Taiwan; DPRK opportunistic provocation; Philippines-Luzon Strait incident; PLAN in the Caribbean. Each move shifts the geographic and political center of gravity off Taiwan and onto a Blue node where the political cost of response is highest and the alliance cohesion fragility is most exposed.

### Pattern C: Civilian-infrastructure pre-positioning as the actual weapon (Moves 2, 9, 10, 18, 22)

A meaningful fraction of the off-distribution surface is *cyber-physical action against civilian critical infrastructure* — CONUS rail and ports; Taiwan LNG regasification; Taiwan power and banking; Taiwan water in drought; TSMC-adjacent fabs. The PLA's "Three Warfares" and "systems collapse" writings (SCMP 2025; PLA *China Military Science* citations) explicitly target civilian chain-collapse nodes. The averaged red-team distribution treats cyber as a supporting fire; the off-distribution insight is that civilian-infrastructure cyber, *combined* with synchronized cognitive and economic effects, can produce most of the coercive effect with much less of the kinetic signature.

### Pattern D: Cognitive-political effect over kinetic effect (Moves 4, 10, 13, 19)

The deepfake triple, the Taishang capital-flight surge, the drone-overflight swarm with cognitive payload, the Kinmen bilateralization gambit — each delivers political dislocation rather than military destruction, and each is structured around a Taiwan domestic-politics seam (KMT-DPP polarization; financial-sector trust; civil-military authority; central-local tension). The averaged red team includes "info ops" but rarely scenarios it as the *primary* effect with synchronized supporting actions. The off-distribution insight is that cognitive moves are not soft addons — they are the main effort when the political-military center of gravity is Taiwan domestic legitimacy.

### Pattern E: Pre-crisis shaping that predetermines the wartime menu (Moves 4, 15, 16, 20, 21)

Several moves do their work months before any declared crisis: Taishang capital pre-positioning; critical-minerals export-control timing; Dongsha EEZ platform-building; LNG contract backchanneling; ADIZ-pattern interceptor depletion. By H-hour, Taiwan and Blue are *already* operating in a degraded posture that the wartime corpus assumes is full. The averaged red team treats "the crisis" as opening at H-hour; the off-distribution insight is that the wartime menu is constrained by 6–24 months of prior shaping, and a competent PRC planner thinks in those time horizons.
