- **Assumption it breaks:** That demonstrative DF-26 fires plus mining of the Miyako/Kerama approaches is an opening of JFSO. Red's framing of it as off-soil shaping forces us to decide whether to read it as Phase 0 or Phase II.

- **Curator's rationale:** B. This is the move our staff is *most* prepared to play and will most enjoy playing — which is why it's not A. The kinetic comfort case. Worth staging because the MCM shortfall is real and underbriefed, but it does not surface the assumptions the rest of this menu does.

## Your move

### Kadena Sortie Suppression via DF-26 Demonstrative Salvo and Mining

Days 1-7: PLARF executes a demonstrative DF-26 salvo into a declared Philippine Sea box 180nm east of Okinawa, paired with PLAN SSN-laid influence-mine field across the Kerama Gap and Miyako Strait approaches to Kadena's reinforcement track. PLAAF H-6K standoff CJ-20 patrols visible on Japanese radar nightly. No strikes on Japanese soil. Goal: make Kadena sortie generation a kinetic risk calculation for Tokyo, not a lawfare one.

#### Actions

- **T+2d** [PLARF Eastern Theater 61st Base] Salvo of 6 DF-26 into pre-declared maritime exclusion box 180nm ENE of Okinawa, 0400L, with NOTAM issued 6 hours prior
  - Target: Philippine Sea exclusion box astride Kadena CSG reinforcement axis
  - Purpose: Establish kinetic precedent that the sortie corridor east of Okinawa is range-held; force Tokyo to weigh aircrew survivability against Article V signaling.
- **T+4d** [PLAN Submarine Force (Type 093B x2)] Covertly lay EM-52 rising mines and influence mines across Miyako Strait and Kerama Gap chokepoints
  - Target: Kadena maritime resupply/reinforcement chokepoints
  - Purpose: Convert the sortie-generation question into a logistics-survivability question; mines are deniable until detonated and force MCM tempo Blue does not have forward.
- **T+1-7d** [PLAAF H-6K + J-16 from Eastern Theater] Nightly CJ-20-armed standoff orbits at 250nm from Okinawa, fire-control radar painting briefly
  - Target: Kadena air defense alert posture
  - Purpose: Force Kadena into continuous DEFCON-elevated alert state; degrade sortie-generation rate through crew-rest and posture friction alone.
- **T+1d** [PLAN Eastern Theater Fleet] Surge 2 SAG including Type 055 east of Yonaguni, AIS on, broadcasting 'live-fire exercise' NAVAREA
  - Target: Waters between Taiwan and Okinawa
  - Purpose: Visibly close the Okinawa-Taiwan operational seam with surface kinetic presence, not lawfare.

#### Intended effect

Suppress Kadena Taiwan-contingency sortie generation by raising the kinetic risk and logistics cost of operating from Okinawa, without striking Japanese soil. If Tokyo restricts sortie generation for force-protection reasons, JFSO problem eases; if not, Beijing has range-held the seam.

#### Why a Red planner could justify this

Anchor: pla-joint-firepower-strike on shaping fires before decisive fires, and pla-df-26-anti-access on holding the second-island-chain reinforcement axis at risk. Serves Goal 2 (no sustained US kinetic) by staying off Japanese soil, and Goal 4 (deterrent demonstration). Relaxes the constraint that kinetic Kadena suppression requires striking Japan — mining and exclusion-box fires achieve it below that threshold.

#### Convergence pattern this move breaks

Breaks the assumption that Kadena is suppressed either by striking it or by political coercion — substitutes a kinetic-but-off-soil shaping campaign that holds the node hostage without crossing the strike threshold.

#### Risks Red accepts

- DF-26 demonstrative salvo is read as opening of JFSO and triggers Article V consultations Beijing wanted to delay
- Mine in Miyako Strait detonates against Japanese-flag commercial hull, producing casus belli
- 093B detected during mine-lay, exposing the covert leg and burning SSN posture
- Tokyo responds by accelerating Kadena dispersal to Iwakuni/Misawa rather than restricting sorties
- CJ-20 orbits provoke JASDF intercept incident with weapons release
