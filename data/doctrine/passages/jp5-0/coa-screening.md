# COA screening criteria — JP 5-0, III-28 (Figure III-6)

> "A valid COA is one that is **adequate, feasible, acceptable, distinguishable, and complete**."
>
> - **Adequate** — Can accomplish the mission within the commander's guidance.
> - **Feasible** — Can accomplish the mission within the established time, space, and resource limitations.
> - **Acceptable** — Must balance cost and risk with the advantage gained.
> - **Distinguishable** — Must be sufficiently different from the other courses of action.
> - **Complete** — Must incorporate objectives, effects, tasks, forces, deployment/employment/sustainment concepts, time estimates, military end state, and mission success criteria.
>
> "The staff should reject potential COAs that do not meet all five criteria."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, Figure III-6, p. III-28)*

> Edition note: JP 5-0 (2020) replaces *adequate* with *suitable*. Both vocabularies are in current use; see `jp5-0-coa-screening-2020` once that passage exists.

## Why this is in the corpus

The primary screening grammar the Blue adjudicator must apply to every candidate Blue response. Each adjudication output should produce a per-criterion verdict. The **distinguishable** criterion in particular flags Blue ensemble outputs that are merely re-skinned versions of one another — a useful mode-collapse detector.
