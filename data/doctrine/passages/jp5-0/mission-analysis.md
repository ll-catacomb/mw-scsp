# Mission Analysis (Step 2) — JP 5-0, III-9 to III-21

> "Mission analysis is critical because it provides direction to the commander and the staff. ... The primary products of mission analysis are a restated mission statement, the commander's intent, planning guidance, the commander's critical information requirements (CCIRs), and an updated [intelligence preparation of the operational environment]."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, p. III-9)*

> "Specified, implied, and essential tasks are derived from a thorough analysis of higher headquarters' orders, the operational environment, and the adversary. ... Assumptions, both friendly and adversary, are reviewed and validated."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, p. III-13 to III-14)*

Mission Analysis produces the inputs every later step inherits: restated mission, commander's intent, the CCIR set (subdivided into Priority Intelligence Requirements / PIRs and Friendly Force Information Requirements / FFIRs), the validated assumption set, and the initial operational approach. JIPOE outputs land here and shape the adversary COA set used in Step 4.

## Why this is in the corpus

Off-distribution Red moves frequently invalidate the *assumption set* the staff settled at this step. A Volt-Typhoon-class action against CONUS rail invalidates the assumption that force flow is intact; a lawfare-as-preface move invalidates the assumption that the operational area's legal predicate is stable; a Hezbollah-holds asymmetric-timing move invalidates the assumption that adversary coordination is tight. Blue adjudication should articulate exactly *which assumption* is now invalid and ask Step 2 to be re-run. Pair with `jp5-0-never-assume-away` when the invalidated assumption is one the staff had assumed away rather than reviewed.
