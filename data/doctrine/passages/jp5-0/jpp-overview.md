# The Joint Planning Process — JP 5-0, III-2 (Fig. III-1)

> "The joint planning process is an orderly, analytical set of logical steps to frame a problem; examine a mission; develop, analyze, and compare alternative courses of action against criteria of success and each other; select the best course of action; and produce a plan or order. ... [JPP] applies to both contingency and crisis planning."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, p. III-1 to III-2)*

The seven steps:

1. **Planning Initiation** — receipt of mission, formation of planning team, initial assessment.
2. **Mission Analysis** *(see `jp5-0-mission-analysis`)* — produce mission statement, commander's intent, planning guidance, CCIRs, restated assumptions.
3. **Course of Action Development** *(see `jp5-0-coa-development`)* — generate multiple plausible COAs.
4. **Course of Action Analysis and Wargaming** *(see `jp5-0-wargaming-action-reaction`, `jp5-0-most-probable-most-dangerous`)* — wargame each COA against adversary COAs derived from JIPOE.
5. **Course of Action Comparison** *(see `jp5-0-coa-comparison`)* — apply evaluation criteria across COAs; recommend.
6. **Course of Action Approval** — commander selects.
7. **Plan or Order Development** — produce OPLAN/CONPLAN/OPORD with branches and sequels.

*(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, Fig. III-1, p. III-2; preserved with renumbering in JP 5-0 (2020), Ch. V.)*

## Why this is in the corpus

Adjudication output should name the step its verdict applies to. Reading a Red move through the JPP lens is the cleanest way to articulate where the move forces Blue to re-execute the planning cycle — the off-distribution moves typically force Blue back to Step 2 (re-frame the problem) rather than Step 5 (compare a new COA). The audit log should record which step the move kicks Blue to.
