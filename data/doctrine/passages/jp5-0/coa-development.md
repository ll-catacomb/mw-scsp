# Course of Action Development (Step 3) — JP 5-0, III-22 to III-28

> "Course of action development is based on selected facts, assumptions, mission, intent, and operational approach. The result is a set of plausible COAs to accomplish the mission. Each developed COA must reflect the level of detail required to differentiate it from the other COAs."
>
> *(JP 5-0, III-22 to III-23)*

> "Each COA must be **suitable, feasible, acceptable, distinguishable, and complete** (see Figure III-6)."
>
> *(JP 5-0, III-28)*

A COA is the macro-organization of an operation: objectives, effects, tasks, and force allocation. Step 3 produces multiple COAs; Step 4 wargames each against the JIPOE-derived adversary COAs (see `jp5-0-wargaming-action-reaction`); Step 5 compares them.

## Why this is in the corpus

Adjudication output should name the step its verdict applies to. A Blue staff that proposes responses to a Red move is, in effect, partial-re-executing Steps 2 → 4 around that move. The audit log records "Blue COA developed under Step 3, screened against the five criteria, wargamed against this Red move as the adversary COA." This is the doctrine vocabulary judges should speak.
