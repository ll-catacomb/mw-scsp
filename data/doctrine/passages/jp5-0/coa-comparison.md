# Course of Action Comparison (Step 5) — JP 5-0, III-32 to III-34

> "COA comparison is a subjective process whereby COAs are considered independently of each other and evaluated/compared against a set of [commander]- and staff-generated evaluation criteria. ... The objective is to identify and recommend the COA that has the highest probability of accomplishing the mission."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, p. III-32)*

> "Evaluation criteria may include, but are not limited to: principles of war; doctrinal fundamentals for the type of operation; commander's guidance and intent; the impact on existing civil and military operations; and the assessment criteria established by the commander."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, p. III-33)*

Comparison is the step where Blue picks among the surviving COAs (those that passed the Step 3 validity screen and were wargamed in Step 4). It uses *evaluation* criteria — commander-selected, often weighted — distinct from the *validity* criteria (suitable / feasible / acceptable / distinguishable / complete) used to screen.

## Why this is in the corpus

Adjudication output should distinguish three failure modes for a Blue response: *fails Step 3 validity* (the COA isn't a valid COA), *loses Step 5 comparison* (the COA is valid but a better one exists), or *passes both but is wargamed badly against this Red move at Step 4*. The audit log lands cleaner when the verdict names the step. Pair with `jp5-0-coa-screening` (validity) and `jp5-0-risk-assessment` (the risk axis often dominates the comparison).
