# Risk assessment in COA analysis — JP 5-0, III-28; III-30

> "Acceptable — Must balance cost and risk with the advantage gained."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, p. III-28; criterion within COA validity)*

> "[COA analysis output should include] (6) A risk assessment."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, p. III-30)*

> "The commander assesses risk in two dimensions: **risk to mission** (the probability that the operation will fail to achieve the desired end state) and **risk to force** (the probability of casualties, equipment loss, or other harm to friendly forces). ... The commander should determine the level of acceptable risk."
>
> *(JP 5-0 (2006), Ch. III, paraphrased framing of risk language)*

Risk decomposes into two axes — *risk to mission* and *risk to force* — and is assessed in two places in the JPP: as a Step 4 wargaming output, and as the substance of the *acceptable* validity criterion in Step 3. A Blue response is rejected as not-acceptable when the cost-and-risk math does not balance the advantage gained, regardless of suitability or feasibility.

## Why this is in the corpus

Every Red-move adjudication should produce a risk-tier verdict on each candidate Blue response: *acceptable*, *acceptable with mitigations*, or *unacceptable*, against both axes. The audit log uses these tiers as the natural attachment for risk fields. Off-distribution Red moves often degrade *risk to force* in ways that compress Blue's acceptable-COA set — a Volt-Typhoon-class action degrades sustainment, raising risk-to-force on every kinetic response Blue might otherwise choose.
