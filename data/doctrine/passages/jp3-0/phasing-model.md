# Six-phase model and the competition continuum — JP 3-0, IV-26 to IV-29

> "Joint operations may be conducted as a series of related major operations and campaigns aimed at achieving strategic and operational objectives within a given time and space. ... To assist commanders and staffs in this regard, six general phases ... have been established for use as needed: Shape (Phase 0), Deter (Phase I), Seize the Initiative (Phase II), Dominate (Phase III), Stabilize (Phase IV), and Enable Civil Authority (Phase V)."
>
> *(JP 3-0 (2008-CH1), Ch. IV, p. IV-26 to IV-27, Fig. IV-7)*

> "The intent of [the Deter] phase is to deter undesirable adversary action by demonstrating the capabilities and resolve of the joint force. ... characterized by preparatory actions that specifically support or facilitate the execution of subsequent phases of the operation/campaign."
>
> *(JP 3-0 (2008-CH1), Ch. IV, p. IV-28)*

The six-phase model is the canonical scaffold against which transition risk and escalation authorities are articulated. Phase boundaries are not just narrative markers — they are the points at which authorities, ROE, force-flow assumptions, and political approvals shift. **Flexible Deterrent Options (FDOs)** are the building blocks of Phase I.

> Edition note: JP 3-0 (2022) replaces the strict six-phase default with a *competition continuum* (cooperation / competition below armed conflict / armed conflict). Both vocabularies remain in current planner usage; the six-phase scaffold is what most senior staffs reach for when describing transition risk.

## Why this is in the corpus

A meaningful class of off-distribution Red moves attacks the *transition* between phases — a quarantine timed to make Phase I Deter slip into Phase II without Blue authorization, a cyber blackout that suppresses the indicators that would normally drive Phase III escalation authorities, or a fait accompli that delivers a Phase III outcome before Blue exits Phase 0. The Blue adjudicator should recognize when a Red move has crossed (or *avoided crossing*) a phase boundary and the audit log should record the boundary it implicates. Pair with `jp3-0-branches-sequels` when the move is best read as a branch trigger.
