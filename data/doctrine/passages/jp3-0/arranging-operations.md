# Arranging operations — JP 3-0, IV-26

> "Commanders determine the best **arrangement of major operations**. This arrangement will often be a combination of simultaneous and sequential operations to achieve the desired end state conditions quickly and at the least cost. ... Phasing assists commanders and staffs to think through the entire operation and to define requirements in terms of forces, resources, time, space, and purpose."
>
> *(JP 3-0, Ch. IV, p. IV-26)*

Arranging operations is the design element that decides *when each thing happens relative to each other thing*. It contains phasing as a sub-construct and is the locus of decisions about simultaneity (do A and B at once), depth (extend operations through the depth of the operational area), timing (clock-time of decisive events), and tempo (rate of operations relative to the adversary's).

## Why this is in the corpus

Several classes of off-distribution Red moves attack *arrangement* rather than any individual element: pre-crisis shaping that pre-degrades Blue's posture before H-hour, ADIZ-driven interceptor depletion that completes before any kinetic act, lawfare predicates emplaced months ahead of the strike. The Blue adjudicator should recognize when a Red move's signature is *its sequencing relative to Blue's plan*, not its kinetic content. Pair with `jp3-0-phasing-model` when the arrangement attack is best read at phase granularity, and with `jp3-0-branches-sequels` when the move triggers a follow-on Blue had not built a branch against.
