# Termination — JP 3-0, IV-7 to IV-9

> "Knowing when to terminate military operations and how to preserve the achieved advantages is a component of strategy and operational art. ... Termination criteria should describe what the [combatant commander] wants the conditions to be in the operational area when operations conclude — both military operations as well as those of other instruments of national power."
>
> *(JP 3-0, Ch. IV, p. IV-8)*

> "The end state and termination criteria flow from the policy goals of the strategic level. ... The conflict termination process must be carefully planned and executed; it should not be left to chance."
>
> *(JP 3-0, Ch. IV, p. IV-7 to IV-8)*

Termination is the element that ties the operation back to the policy objective. Without an articulated end state and termination criteria, the operation has no doctrinal stopping rule, and Phase IV (Stabilize) and Phase V (Enable Civil Authority) become improvisations rather than the conclusion of the plan.

## Why this is in the corpus

A high-leverage class of off-distribution Red moves does not seek battlefield victory at all — it seeks to make Blue's *termination criteria* unattainable or politically intolerable. A Pratas-with-conditional-offer move, a hostage-and-ransom outlying-island seizure, or a humanitarian-corridor pretext at the UNSC each functions by offering Blue a "termination" that is structurally worse than continued conflict. Blue adjudication should retrieve this passage when the move is best understood as an attack on the end-state-and-termination scaffold rather than on military forces. Pair with `jp3-0-phasing-model` when the move targets the Phase IV/V transition specifically.
