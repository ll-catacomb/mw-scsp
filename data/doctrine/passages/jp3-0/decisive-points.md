# Decisive points — JP 3-0, IV-12

> "A decisive point is a geographic place, specific key event, critical factor, or function that, when acted upon, allows a commander to gain a marked advantage over an adversary or contributes materially to achieving success... Decisive points can be thought of as a way to relate what is 'critical' to what is 'vulnerable.'"
>
> *(JP 3-0, IV-12)*

A decisive point is the operational-level analogue of a critical vulnerability: where Blue can act to materially disrupt Red's path to its objectives. The doctrinal frame assumes the set of decisive points is enumerable and stable — a planning artifact derived from JIPOE.

## Why this is in the corpus

This is one of the highest-leverage passages for the off-distribution-flag stage. An interesting class of off-distribution Red moves *redefines* the decisive-point set: it makes a previously-unconsidered geographic place, key event, or function suddenly load-bearing for Blue. (Examples: subsea-cable cuts in the Eastern Mediterranean during the cascade window; an Okinawan municipal vote during an INDOPACOM crisis; a Taiwanese port-labor union strike timed to a quarantine.) The Blue adjudicator should be able to recognize when a Red move has shifted the decisive-point set, and the audit log should flag the shift.
