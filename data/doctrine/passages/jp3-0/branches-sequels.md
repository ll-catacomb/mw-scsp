# Branches and sequels — JP 3-0, IV-19 and Exec Summary p. xx

> "The CONOPS describes how the actions of the joint force components and supporting organizations will be integrated, synchronized, and phased to accomplish the mission, including potential branches and sequels."
>
> *(JP 3-0, Executive Summary, p. xx)*

A **branch** is a contingent option built into a base plan to address an alternative situation that may arise during execution — a change in the disposition, composition, or direction of friendly forces; a change in the enemy or the operational environment; or a change in mission. A **sequel** is the major operation that follows the current one, typically responding to its outcome (victory, stalemate, defeat). Branches and sequels exist so that the plan can absorb foreseen contingencies without requiring a return to the full Joint Planning Process.

## Why this is in the corpus

Every Red move adjudicated by Blue should produce a verdict naming at least one branch or sequel implication: *which branch in the existing plan does this Red move trigger, and which sequel does it demand?* The audit log uses this as a doctrine-fidelity check. Off-distribution Red moves often have the property that they trigger *no existing branch* — the branch set was developed under JIPOE assumptions the move escapes. Pair with `jp5-0-most-probable-most-dangerous` when the move falls outside the most-probable / most-dangerous COA pair the branches were built against.
