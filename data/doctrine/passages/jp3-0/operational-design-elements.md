# Elements of operational design — JP 3-0, Fig. IV-3, IV-3 to IV-21

> "The elements of operational design are tools to help the [joint force commander] and staff visualize and shape the operational approach. ... A commander selects from these elements based on the unique requirements of the mission and operational environment."
>
> *(JP 3-0, Ch. IV, paraphrased framing of Fig. IV-3)*

The seventeen elements a Blue planner is expected to consider:

1. **Termination** — conditions under which the operation concludes
2. **End state and objectives**
3. **Effects** — desired and undesired
4. **Center of gravity** *(see `jp3-0-cog`)*
5. **Decisive points** *(see `jp3-0-decisive-points`)*
6. **Direct vs. indirect approach**
7. **Lines of operations and lines of effort** *(see `jp3-0-lines-of-operations`)*
8. **Operational reach**
9. **Simultaneity and depth**
10. **Timing and tempo**
11. **Forces and functions**
12. **Leverage**
13. **Balance**
14. **Anticipation**
15. **Synergy**
16. **Culmination**
17. **Arranging operations** *(includes phasing — see `jp3-0-phasing-model`, `jp3-0-arranging-operations`)*

## Why this is in the corpus

The element list is the working vocabulary the Blue adjudicator must speak. Each adjudication output should reference at least one element by name. Off-distribution moves that the modal ensemble misses often map onto the *less-frequently-cited* elements — leverage, anticipation, culmination, arranging — because those elements articulate effects the kinetic-only frame flattens. A move that forces *early culmination* of a Blue posture, that produces an *indirect approach* to a friendly COG, or that re-arranges *timing and tempo* in a way no existing branch absorbs is doctrine-grounded even when it is off-distribution.
