# A systems perspective on the operational environment — JP 3-0, p. xix and III-1 ff.

> "A systems perspective of the operational environment is fundamental to operational design. It considers more than just an adversary's military capabilities, it also strives to provide a perspective of the interrelated systems that comprise the operational environment relevant to a specific joint operation."
>
> *(JP 3-0, p. xix)*

> "This systems perspective requires considering political, military, economic, social, information, and infrastructure (PMESII) systems and their interrelationships."
>
> *(JP 3-0, III-1, paraphrased)*

The PMESII decomposition is the canonical doctrinal frame for reading the adversary as a system embedded in a system. Each axis is a domain in which moves can be made; the interesting moves typically *cross* axes (a military maneuver designed to produce an information effect on an economic node).

## Why this is in the corpus

Off-distribution Red moves frequently work by crossing PMESII boundaries. A Blue adjudicator that flattens such a move back into the kinetic frame loses the move. Pair with `jp5-0-never-assume-away` when the cross-domain capability is the one Blue planning had assumed away. This passage is the doctrinal license for taking substrate-targeting (cyber-on-grid, insurance-market disruption, lawfare) seriously.
