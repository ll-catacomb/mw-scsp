# Lines of operations and lines of effort — JP 3-0, IV-13 to IV-16

> "A **line of operations** defines the directional orientation of a force in time and space in relation to the enemy. It connects the force with its base of operations and its objectives. ... A force operates on **interior lines** when its operations diverge from a central point. Interior lines usually represent shorter distances than exterior lines. **Exterior lines**, in contrast, focus operations on or against an enemy from several directions."
>
> *(JP 3-0, Ch. IV, p. IV-13)*

> "**Lines of effort** link multiple tasks and missions using the logic of purpose — cause and effect — to focus efforts toward establishing operational and strategic conditions. ... Commanders may use lines of effort to describe an operation in terms of decisive points that, when integrated, accomplish desired effects."
>
> *(JP 3-0, Ch. IV, p. IV-15)*

LOOs describe the *physical* arrangement of forces relative to objectives; LOEs describe the *logical* arrangement of tasks relative to effects. The two are usually combined in a single graphic that shows physical-line and logical-line work in parallel.

## Why this is in the corpus

Off-distribution moves frequently work along an LOE that the modal ensemble has not articulated — a *cognitive* line of effort, a *legal* line of effort, an *insurance-market* line of effort. Naming the LOE the move operates on helps the Blue adjudicator avoid flattening it into a kinetic-LOO frame. Pair with `jp3-0-decisive-points` when the move shifts the decisive-point set along an LOE Blue had not built tasks against.
