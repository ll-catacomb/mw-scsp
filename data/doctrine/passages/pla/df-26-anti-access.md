# DF-26 IRBM and DF-21D ASBM: counter-intervention anchors — DoD CMPR; CSIS; Mihal

The two PLA Rocket Force systems most often cited as the technical foundation of the PRC counter-intervention envelope:

**DF-26 (Dong Feng-26) — intermediate-range ballistic missile.** Range ~4,000 km. Conventional/nuclear dual-capable. Open-source reporting describes maneuverable reentry vehicle (MaRV) variants. Inventory anchor (DoD CMPR 2024): ~500 conventional IRBMs in the DF-26 family with ~250–400 launchers across multiple PLARF brigades. The **"Guam killer"** label originated with the system's range-to-Guam from PLARF garrisons in central and western China; CSIS ChinaPower documents the regional reach extending to Andersen AFB.

**DF-21D — anti-ship ballistic missile (ASBM).** Range ~1,500 km, terminal MaRV claimed. The **"carrier killer"** label originated with the system's design intent against US Navy CSGs operating in the Western Pacific. CSIS ChinaPower / DoD CMPR document inventory growth from ~6 launchers in 2013 to ~30 in 2020.

CSIS analysis cited in *Newsweek* (2025) finds **36 missiles can shut Kadena to fighter operations for four days.** The doctrinal logic: in a Taiwan contingency, the DF-26 / DF-21D inventory holds the US/allied forward-base set (Kadena, Iwakuni, Andersen, Misawa, Yokota) and the deployed CSG at risk in the first 24–72 hours, suppressing Blue's intervention posture during the JFSO and JILC initial waves.

**Adjacent PLARF systems frequently cited in the modal corpus** (DoD CMPR 2024; CSIS ChinaPower):
- ~900 conventional SRBMs (DF-15, DF-16 family)
- ~1,300 conventional MRBMs
- ~400 GLCMs
- DF-17 hypersonic glide vehicle deployed to at least three brigades (CSIS ChinaPower)

## Why this is in the corpus

The modal LLM ensemble will reach for these systems when generating PLA opening moves that include counter-intervention strikes against Guam, Kadena, Yokota, Iwakuni, or deployed CSGs. Adjudicators should retrieve this passage to validate the technical envelope (range, inventory, launcher count) the modal move is implicitly assuming. Off-distribution variants typically *withhold* DF-21D against CSGs (preserving the ambiguity that constrains US carrier ops) or *substitute* DF-26 strikes on Japan-based logistics for strikes on Taiwan itself (RA-3 Move 3). Pair with `pla-joint-firepower-strike` (the campaign-level concept these systems execute within) and `pla-joint-island-landing-campaign` (the JILC's preliminary-operations phase).
