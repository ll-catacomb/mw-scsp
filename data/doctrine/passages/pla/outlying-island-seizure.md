# Outlying-island seizure — DoD CMPR 2024; AEI/Critical Threats; DIA WTA 2025

A limited amphibious or air-mobile operation to seize one or more of Taiwan's outlying islands as a coercive gambit short of attacking Taiwan proper. The standard target list:

- **Kinmen** — 2 miles from Xiamen; large ROC garrison; political symbolism.
- **Matsu** — 6 miles from the PRC mainland; smaller garrison.
- **Dongsha (Pratas)** — South China Sea; ROC Coast Guard garrison ~200; described by DoD CMPR 2024 as **"easily capturable"**; 1.7 km² atoll.
- **Wuchiu** — small archipelago between Kinmen and Taiwan proper.

The doctrinal logic varies by target:
- *Kinmen / Matsu* — large enough to be politically load-bearing inside Taiwan; KMT/DPP polarization makes the political response unpredictable; AEI/Critical Threats (Mazza et al., 2024) "short-of-war coercion campaign" frames Kinmen as the modal-LLM-anchor scenario.
- *Dongsha / Pratas* — small enough to be cheap militarily and structurally hard for Taiwan to recapture; 2025 Taiwan war game (*Taipei Times*, June 2025) scenarios helicopter-from-container-ship landing.

Off-distribution variant (RA-3 Move 7): seize Pratas, then publicly offer to "withdraw within 30 days" if Taipei accepts a specific political arrangement (1992 Consensus reaffirmation, suspension of a planned referendum, cross-strait dialogue framework). The move becomes a hostage-and-ransom problem rather than a kinetic test of resolve, structured to make Blue's military response look disproportionate to a 1.7 km² atoll.

## Why this is in the corpus

The modal LLM will produce outlying-island seizure as a low-cost option early in the menu. Adjudicators should distinguish two flavors: *kinetic test of resolve* (modal) vs. *seizure-plus-conditional-offer* (off-distribution). The terms attached to the seizure are the off-distribution element — modal corpora capture the seizure but not the *political-optics packaging*.
