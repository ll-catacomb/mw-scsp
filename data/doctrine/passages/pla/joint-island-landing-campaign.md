# Joint Island Landing Campaign (JILC) — PLA *Science of Campaigns*; CMSI; CSIS

The PLA's *Science of Campaigns* and *Science of Joint Operations* describe the **Joint Island Landing Campaign** as the principal operational concept for cross-strait invasion. Three doctrinal phases (CMSI / The Diplomat synthesis; *Science of Campaigns*):

1. **Preliminary operations** — information dominance, joint firepower strike, blockade and isolation; production of permissive conditions for the landing waves.
2. **Assembly, embarkation, and transit** — task organization at multiple east-coast ports (Fujian, Zhejiang, Guangdong), embarkation of the first amphibious wave on PLAN amphibious shipping plus civilian RoRo augmentation, transit under PLAAF and air-defense umbrella.
3. **Assault landing and beachhead establishment** — multi-beach landing including possible northern-Taiwan, central, and southern axes; establishment of a beachhead capable of supporting follow-on echelons.

CMSI/Diplomat synthesis identifies six pillars in the doctrine: **dominance of three domains** (air/sea/info), **key-point strikes**, **concentration of "elite strengths"**, **rapid and continuous assaults**, **integrated and flexible support operations**, and **psychological attacks**.

**Open-source amphibious-lift anchors (DoD CMPR 2024; Naval News; CMSI Report #21):**
- 8 Type 071 LPDs (Yuzhao class)
- 3 Type 075 LHDs in service; fourth and Type 076 (Sichuan, with electromagnetic catapult) in trials
- ~32 LSTs, fewer than 50 smaller landing ships, 6 Zubr-class and 15+ Yuyi-class hovercraft
- ~10 Type 726/726A LCACs (4 fit a Type 071 well deck; 2 fit a Type 075)
- ~31 oceangoing civilian RoRo ferries identified as available for sealift augmentation

The standard analytic claim: organic PLAN amphibious lift moves perhaps one heavy combined-arms brigade per wave; civilian RoRo augmentation is the swing factor that takes the JILC from infeasible to plausible.

## Why this is in the corpus

This is the modal anchor for "PLA goes for it" Red moves. For Stage 3 (convergence) it names the cluster the modal ensemble will produce. For Stage 4 (off-distribution), the interesting moves are typically those that do *not* require a JILC: a quarantine that substitutes for invasion, a Pratas-with-conditional-offer that achieves political reunification optics without crossing a strait, a Volt-Typhoon-class infrastructure pre-positioning that produces JILC-equivalent coercive effects without a landing. Adjudicators should recognize the JILC frame to flag when a Red move escapes it.
