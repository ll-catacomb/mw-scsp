# Joint Firepower Strike Operation (JFSO)

The PLA's *Science of Joint Operations* identifies the **Joint Firepower Strike Operation** as the campaign concept for concentrated long-range strikes by the PLA Rocket Force, PLAAF, and PLAN cruise-missile platforms against an adversary's command and control, air bases, naval bases, radars, integrated air defenses, and critical civilian infrastructure (power, water, ports). Open-source analytic syntheses (DoD CMPR 2024; CASI; CSIS ChinaPower; Mihal, *Military Review*, 2021) describe the JFSO as designed to *"intimidate an adversary's leadership and population, break its will to resist, and force it to abandon or reverse its strategic intentions."*

Open-source inventory anchors as of 2024 (DoD CMPR 2024; CSIS ChinaPower):

- ~900 conventional short-range ballistic missiles (SRBM)
- ~1,300 conventional medium-range ballistic missiles (MRBM)
- ~500 conventional intermediate-range ballistic missiles (IRBM), DF-26 family
- ~400 ground-launched cruise missiles (GLCM)
- DF-17 hypersonic glide vehicle deployed to at least three brigades
- DF-21D ASBM growing from ~6 to ~30 launchers between 2013 and 2020

JFSO can be executed as an independent escalation move or as the opening salvo of an invasion (Joint Island Landing Campaign).

## Why this is in the corpus

This is the heaviest modal anchor in the Taiwan corpus — the modal LLM ensemble will produce some variant of "open with a JFSO-class salvo" with high probability. For Stage 3 (convergence), this passage names the cluster. For Stage 4 (off-distribution), the interesting moves are usually those that *do not* open with JFSO: a quarantine timed to substitute for the salvo, a Volt-Typhoon-class infrastructure pre-positioning that produces JFSO-equivalent effects without a strike, a coalition-fragmentation move that makes the salvo politically unaffordable. Adjudicators should be able to recognize the JFSO frame to flag when a Red move escapes it.
