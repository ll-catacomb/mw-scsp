# Active Defense — PLA *Science of Military Strategy*; Cole; Fravel

**Active Defense** (积极防御, *jījí fángyù*) is the umbrella doctrinal frame the PLA uses to characterize its strategic posture. The frame is canonical across post-1956 PLA writings and is reaffirmed in the AMS *Science of Military Strategy* (2013, 2020 editions). Two doctrinal slogans capture its operative content:

> "**Strategically defensive, operationally offensive.**" — The state's *strategic* posture is defensive (PRC does not initiate war for territorial expansion; the doctrine is reactive to a perceived hostile move). At the *operational* level, the PLA preempts and seizes initiative once the strategic threshold is judged crossed.
>
> "**Gain mastery by striking afterwards** (后发制人, *hòu fā zhì rén*)." — The PLA acts second in name and first in effect: the doctrinal frame allows offensive operational action once an adversary has crossed a (PRC-defined) strategic line, with the action characterized as a *response* to that crossing.

(*Synthesis from* AMS, *Science of Military Strategy* (2013/2020); Bernard Cole, *China's Quest for Great Power* (2016); M. Taylor Fravel, *Active Defense: China's Military Strategy Since 1949* (Princeton, 2019); CASI summaries.)

The frame's operational consequence is that the **first kinetic act** by the PLA is consistently framed by Beijing as *defensive*. Taiwan independence declaration, US recognition shift, an arms-package crossing some declared red line — each is doctrinally adequate as the strategic-defensive trigger that licenses operationally offensive PLA action. The frame is not cynical packaging; it is the doctrinal logic the planning system runs on.

## Why this is in the corpus

The Blue adjudicator should articulate which side of the active-defense frame a Red move is being parsed under. Off-distribution moves often *exploit the frame asymmetry*: the move is defensive in PRC self-presentation (responding to a Taiwan referendum, a US arms package, a Japan-US joint exercise) while being operationally offensive in Western analytic reading. The audit log should record both the PRC self-framing and the Western framing; the gap between them is itself a doctrinal datapoint.
