# Decapitation strike — Easton; Project 2049; SCMP

Concentrated strikes — combinations of cruise/ballistic missiles, cyber, special operations forces, and (post-2023) drones — against Taiwan's senior political-military leadership, communications nodes, and key government facilities. Open-source documentation:

- PLA training facilities in northern China include replicas of Taiwan's **Presidential Office Building**, photographed from CCTV imagery (CCTV 2015; updated SCMP reporting 2020–2024 documents the training area expanded threefold since 2020).
- Easton (*The Chinese Invasion Threat*, Project 2049 / Camphor Press, 2017; *Hostile Harbors*, 2021) treats decapitation as the canonical opening of a "short, sharp" invasion variant.
- Easton's framing: if decapitation fails, the PLA may need to land 300,000–400,000 troops to seize key objectives — the cost of failed decapitation is the JILC at full scale.

The doctrinal logic: a successful decapitation collapses Taiwan command-and-control during the window in which the PLA is conducting JFSO and amphibious assembly, increasing the probability that the JILC's first wave faces a degraded Taiwan response. The political logic: a decapitated Taiwan presents the international community with a *fait accompli* that frames the conflict as already over.

## Why this is in the corpus

The modal LLM ensemble will produce decapitation as a high-probability opening when the scenario allows kinetic action, anchored on Easton's open-source documentation. Adjudicators should recognize the move and flag whether the Red ensemble has bundled it with a JFSO + JILC sequence (the canonical packaging) or proposed it as standalone (rarer, and a sign the Red ensemble is reaching for "short, sharp" framings). Pair with `pla-joint-firepower-strike` (the kinetic envelope) and `pla-joint-island-landing-campaign` (the follow-on the decapitation is meant to enable).
