# Iran's April 13–14, 2024 strike on Israel — CSIS; CENTCOM/IDF readouts

The April 13–14, 2024 Iranian direct strike on Israel is the canonical open-source anchor for the **telegraphed-proportional retaliation** template. The strike was sized as Iran's response to the April 1, 2024 Israeli strike on the Iranian consulate compound in Damascus, which killed senior IRGC-Quds Force officers including Brig. Gen. Mohammad Reza Zahedi.

**Open-source salvo composition** (CSIS, "Iran's Calibrated Strike on Israel," April 2024; US CENTCOM and IDF press readouts):
- ~120 ballistic missiles
- ~30 cruise missiles
- ~170 one-way attack drones (predominantly Shahed-136 / Geran-2 family)
- The largest single drone attack in history at the time

**Defensive performance** (CENTCOM/IDF readouts; CSIS retrospectives):
- Israel intercepted >99% of inbound projectiles
- US CENTCOM downed ~80 projectiles directly
- Allied contributions (UK Typhoons, French air defense over Jordan, Jordanian air defense) materially shaped outcome

**Telegraphing characteristics**:
- Notification through Swiss, Omani, and Turkish backchannels reportedly preceded the launches by 24–72 hours
- Iran issued public statements throughout suggesting the strike was deliberately *contained* and that further escalation would be conditional on Israeli response
- Strike timing avoided concentrated population centers and was structured to permit successful interception

The strike's signature property is that it delivered a *visible* and *attributable* response while remaining on a rung Iran could plausibly characterize as proportional. The post-strike rhetorical framing was the strike's most important feature: "the matter can be deemed concluded."
