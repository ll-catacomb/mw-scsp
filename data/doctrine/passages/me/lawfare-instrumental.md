# Lawfare-instrumental and influence-instrumental moves — RA-4 Cluster B/F

A class of off-distribution Red moves whose principal effect is on **Blue's decision clock and political surface** rather than on military forces or critical infrastructure. The canonical instances from RA-4's Cluster B (escape-the-kinetic-register) and Cluster F (target-the-blue-political-surface):

**Procedural lawfare at the UNSC**:
- Iranian (or Iranian-aligned) introduction of a humanitarian-corridor demand at the UNSC during the 96-hour response window
- Pairs with allied procedural support (Russia, China; selected non-aligned states) to extend Security Council debate timeline
- Forces Western capitals into "ceasefire vs. defense" decisions while strikes continue
- The diplomatic clock weaponized against the kinetic clock

**US-domestic litigation-instrumental warfare**:
- Iranian declaratory posture inviting US-domestic litigation against US strike operations
- AUMF / War Powers Resolution arguments surfaced in US courts by US-resident legal actors coordinated with the Iranian declaratory posture
- Effect: extend the political response window long enough that Iran's reconstitution timeline benefits
- Modal red teams do not parse this as a "move" because the effect is procedural, not kinetic

**Influence-operation timing on the Blue political surface**:
- Iranian or Iranian-aligned release of US-government-classified or US-private-sector-classified material during the response window
- Timing calibrated to a Sunday-talk-show / Congressional-hearing news cycle
- Effect: constrain executive action through legislative noise; force the executive into defensive-political posture during the window in which decisive action is otherwise available
- Precedent: 2024–2025 cyber operations against US political campaigns documented by Microsoft Threat Intelligence and CISA advisories

**Classified-release timing as a force multiplier on existing kinetic action** — even small kinetic strikes (a Tower-22-class casualty event below the line that triggered the February 2024 response) become harder to respond to when paired with a synchronized influence-operation release; the political-noise floor rises, and the executive's response window narrows.

## Why this is in the corpus

The averaged LLM red-team treats influence operations as adjacent to kinetic action — a supporting fire, not a main effort. The off-distribution insight is that for an Iranian (or Iranian-aligned) actor whose strategic constraint is *time to reconstitute*, the high-leverage move is to *extend Blue's decision window* by procedural and litigation-instrumental means, while the kinetic component stays small or absent. Adjudicators should retrieve this passage when a Red move's principal effect is articulated against Blue's decision clock or political surface, not against military forces or infrastructure. The doctrine vocabulary the move escapes is the entire COA-development frame — Blue cannot wargame a court filing against a "most probable adversary COA." Pair with `me-iran-october-2024-strike` (the kinetic context the lawfare-instrumental move is timed against) and `jp5-0-never-assume-away` (the planning failure mode this exposes — Blue planning had assumed the political-surface posture would hold).
